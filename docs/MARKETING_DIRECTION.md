# Marketing Direction — Vestigio

*Last updated: 2026-04-19*

## The Core Problem With Our Current Copy

> "We're explaining the product, not selling the decision." — Marketing team

Our site communicates: **audit → analysis → discovery**
Our product delivers: **clarity → action → financial impact**

This is a value downgrade. We're positioning a decision engine as a tool.

---

## Two Surfaces, Two Jobs

### Homepage (/)

| Attribute | Definition |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Audience** | Organic, referral, brand search, curious operators |
| **Awareness** | Low — they may not know they have a problem |
| **Job** | Create the category. Plant the seed that they're bleeding money without knowing it. Build authority. |
| **Tone** | Authoritative confidence. McKinsey clarity, Stripe restraint. |
| **Pressure** | Intellectual, not emotional. Make them think, not panic. |
| **CTA weight** | Low — "Start free" or "See your first report" |
| **Feature depth** | Minimal — outcomes only, process hidden |

### Landing Page (/lp)

| Attribute | Definition |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Audience** | Paid traffic, ad click-throughs, retargeting |
| **Awareness** | Medium-high — they clicked an ad with a financial promise |
| **Job** | Convert. Fast. One action. Match the ad promise and deliver proof. |
| **Tone** | Direct confrontation. Financial, specific, urgent. |
| **Pressure** | High — dollars are leaving NOW, every day without action is loss |
| **CTA weight** | High — "See your first report in 24h" |
| **Feature depth** | Zero — only outcomes + proof + CTA |

## Copy Psychology Principles

### 1. Loss Aversion > Gain Framing

People feel losses 2x more intensely than equivalent gains (Kahneman & Tversky).

**Current (gain frame):** "Recover $67.2k/month across 9 ranked actions"
**Better (loss frame):** "You're losing $67.2k/month. Here are the 9 places it's happening."

### 2. Specificity = Credibility

Vague claims feel like marketing. Specific numbers feel like data.

**Weak:** "Find revenue leaks across your funnel"
**Strong:** "Your checkout takes 2.4s longer than it should. That's costing you $18k–$42k/month."

### 3. The "Already Happening" Frame

Don't present the problem as hypothetical. Present it as ongoing.

**Hypothetical:** "What if your funnel has hidden problems?"
**Already happening:** "Your funnel has problems right now. Vestigio shows you which ones and what they cost."

### 4. Consequence Before Solution

The problem needs to hurt before the solution feels valuable.

**Current order:** Here's what Vestigio does → Here's the impact
**Better order:** Here's what's costing you money → Here's how Vestigio stops it

### 5. Identity-Based Positioning

Don't describe users. Describe who they become.

**Feature-based:** "Vestigio gives you prioritized actions"
**Identity-based:** "Operators who use Vestigio don't guess. They decide."

## Section-by-Section Rewrites

### Hero Section

**CURRENT:**
```
Skip the analytics.
What matters is the decision.

Vestigio audits your digital operation automatically and ranks 
what to fix so you stop leaving money on the table.
```

**PROPOSED (Homepage):**
```
Your funnel is leaking.
Vestigio shows you where and how much.

See every revenue leak, ranked by financial impact, 
with browser-verified evidence. First report in 24 hours.
```

**PROPOSED (Landing Page):**
```
SaaS companies lose $38k/month
to problems they can't see.

Enter your domain. See yours in 24 hours.
[Run Free Diagnostic]
```

**Why:** The homepage version states the problem (leaking) and the value (where + how much) in two lines. No process explanation. The LP version leads with the financial pain, makes it about THEIR money, and goes straight to CTA.

---

### Sub-Hero / Social Proof Strip

**CURRENT:** (not present above fold)

**PROPOSED:** Move financial proof above the fold.

```
Average first report: 9 findings, $41k/month in recoverable revenue.
```

One line. Specific. Creates the "what if that's me?" reaction before they scroll.

---

### Product Tour Section

**CURRENT:**
```
Not a dashboard. A queue of decisions.
Every tab is a different lens on the same revenue picture.
```

**PROPOSED:**
```
Not a dashboard. A queue of decisions.
This is what your first report looks like.
```

**Why:** "Every tab is a different lens on the same revenue picture" is product-speak. The visitor wants to know what THEY will see, not how the product is architectured. "This is what your first report looks like" creates anticipation and makes it personal.

---

### Features / Bento Section

**CURRENT STRUCTURE:**
1. Action Queue (what it does)
2. Revenue Leaks (what it finds)
3. Evidence Trail (how it verifies)
4. Continuous Watch (how it monitors)

**PROPOSED STRUCTURE:**
1. "Here's what's costing you money" (the problem, financial)
2. "Here's what to fix first" (the action, prioritized)
3. "Here's the proof" (evidence, one line)
4. "Here's what changed since yesterday" (continuous, urgency)

**Why:** Same information, reframed from product capabilities to user outcomes. Each section starts with the user's situation, not Vestigio's feature.

---

### Use Cases Section

**Why:** Real questions from real personas. The visitor sees themselves in the scenario. Then the answer is simple: Vestigio handles it.

### MiniCalc / Diagnostic Section

**CURRENT POSITION:** Below the fold, after features
**PROPOSED POSITION:** Second section on the page (right after hero)

**Why:** "What you're leaving on the table" is polite. "How much are you losing right now?" is confrontational. The second version creates urgency and implies the loss is happening NOW.

## Words to Use vs Avoid

### Use

| Word/Phrase | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Decision | We sell decisions, not data |
| Revenue / dollars / $X/month | Financial specificity > vague severity |
| Evidence / proof / verified | Differentiates from LLM-generated guesses |
| Queue / ranked / first | Implies prioritization and action |
| Recover / stop losing / protect | Loss aversion frame |
| Clarity | The emotional benefit of our product |
| In 24 hours / this week | Speed and urgency |
| Your funnel / your checkout / your revenue | Make it personal |

### Avoid

| Word/Phrase | Why | Replace with |
|-------------|-----|--------------|
| Audit | Sounds like compliance, passive | "Diagnose" or "Find" |
| Analytics | Positions us as GA4 competitor | "Decision engine" or "Intelligence" |
| Insights | Vague, every tool claims this | "Findings with dollar amounts" |
| Optimize / Streamline | Empty buzzwords | Specific outcome ("fix", "recover") |
| Innovative / AI-powered | Overused, means nothing | Show the AI working, don't label it |
| Platform | Generic SaaS word | "Decision engine" or just "Vestigio" |
| Monitor | Sounds like uptime tool | "Watch" or "Catch" |
| Comprehensive / Robust | Filler adjectives | Remove entirely |
| Solution | The most meaningless word in SaaS | Name the specific thing |
| Discover | Passive, no urgency | "See" or "Find" (active) |

## Page Structure: Current vs Proposed

### Homepage (/)

**CURRENT ORDER:**
1. Hero (headline + subtitle)
2. Product Tour (tabs with demo)
3. Counter / Quick Stats
4. Features (4 bento cards)
5. Use Cases (3 layers)
6. MiniCalc diagnostic
7. Client Gallery
8. Success Stories
9. FAQ
10. Final CTA

**PROPOSED ORDER:**
1. Hero (financial headline + sub)
2. Social proof strip (1 line: average first report stats)
3. MiniCalc diagnostic (moved UP — from position 6 to position 3)
4. Product Tour ("This is what your first report looks like")
5. Outcome cards (reframed from features to user outcomes)
6. Use case scenarios (persona questions, not feature layers)
7. Proof (real testimonials or remove section)
8. FAQ (3 questions max, simplified)
9. Final CTA (urgency + financial)

**Key changes:**
- MiniCalc moves from position 6 to position 3 (interactive engagement before explanation)
- Features reframed as outcomes
- Success stories either get real or get removed
- FAQ dramatically shortened

### Landing Page (/lp)

**PROPOSED ORDER:**
1. Financial headline + CTA (number first, headline second)
2. MiniCalc (immediate interaction)
3. "What your first report shows" (3 screenshots, no explanation)
4. One testimonial (or one stat)
5. Final CTA (repeat)

Total scroll: 2 screens max. No features, no process, no FAQ.

## Pricing Copy Direction

**CURRENT:**
```
Veja exatamente o que está perdendo. Corrija. Escale.
Inteligência que se paga sozinha.
```

**PROPOSED:**
```
Pick the plan that matches your revenue at risk.
Every plan pays for itself in the first cycle.
```

**Why:** "Matches your revenue at risk" frames the pricing decision as risk-proportional, not feature-proportional. "Pays for itself in the first cycle" is the 4X ROI guarantee restated as a promise.

**Tier descriptions should emphasize outcome, not feature count:**

| Current | Proposed |
|---------|----------|
| "Essential intelligence for small teams getting started" | "See what's costing you money. Fix the top 3." |
| "Full analysis suite for growing businesses that need an edge" | "Full financial clarity across 3 environments. Daily." |
| "Unlimited scale with dedicated support for large organizations" | "Enterprise-grade. 10 environments. Dedicated support. SLA." |

## Measuring Success

After implementing these changes, track:

1. **Hero → MiniCalc conversion** — do more people enter their domain?
2. **MiniCalc → Signup conversion** — does the new position increase completions?
3. **Time on page** — shorter is better (they're deciding, not reading)
4. **Bounce rate by section** — where do people stop scrolling?
5. **/lp conversion rate** — should be 2-3x higher than homepage

## Summary

The site should make visitors feel one thing: **"I need to check if this is happening to me."**

Not: "This is a cool tool."
Not: "Interesting technology."
Not: "Maybe I'll try this later."

**"I need to check right now."**

Every word, every section, every CTA should drive toward that single reaction.
